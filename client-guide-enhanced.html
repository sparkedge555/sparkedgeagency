First Contact & Lead Qualification

Someone reached out. Now you qualify them fast and move them forward.

The 30-minute rule

Respond within 30 minutes during business hours. Every hour you wait, conversion probability drops by ~15%. Speed is your #1 trust signal as a solo founder — it shows you're serious, attentive, and reliable. Set a phone notification for any new WhatsApp message.

First reply template (inbound inquiry)

📲 First Reply — Inbound WhatsApp Lead Hi [Name]! 👋 Thanks for reaching out to SparkEdge. I'm Raunak — I personally handle all projects here, so you're talking directly to the person who'll build your website. Quick question to get started: What kind of business do you have, and what are you looking to build? (e.g., 5-page coaching website, landing page, redesign) This helps me give you a proper recommendation right away. 🚀

Qualify the lead — 3 key questions

Ask these before investing time in a call or proposal. One message, three questions naturally woven in:

  • What's your business? — Determines package, effort, and price point
  • What's your timeline? — "ASAP" = motivated buyer. "Someday" = nurture.
  • Do you have a budget in mind? — Optional, but filters serious buyers fast
📲 Qualification Message (after first reply) Perfect! A few quick things to give you the best recommendation: 1. What's your business exactly? (coaching, school, clinic, brand?) 2. Do you need a fresh website or a redesign of an existing one? 3. When would you like to go live — any timeline in mind? (No pressure on budget yet — just helps me suggest the right package 🙂)

Lead scoring — know who to prioritize

Hot Lead (call TODAY)

Knows what they want · Has a deadline · Mentions budget · Asked for price directly · Replies quickly · Has an existing business

Warm Lead (nurture)

"Just exploring" · No timeline · Student starting a business · Hasn't replied in 24h · Price is first question before needs

Discovery Call Script

15–20 minutes that turn a stranger into a convinced buyer.

Call structure — minute by minute

0–2 min · Warm Up

Open with their world, not yours

"Thanks for the time — I'll keep this under 20 minutes. Tell me a bit about your business. How long have you been running it, and what's been going well lately?"

Let them talk. This builds rapport and gives you data for your pitch.

2–7 min · Diagnose

Find the exact gap — in their words

"What made you reach out now? Are you currently getting enquiries from anywhere — Google, Instagram, word of mouth?"

Listen carefully. The words they use to describe the gap = the words you use in your proposal. Mirror their language back to them.

7–12 min · Present

Solution in 3 specific points, tied to their problem

"Based on what you've told me, here's exactly what I'd build and why:" — Then give 3 concrete features connected to their stated problems. E.g. "You said parents search on Google — so we'd add local SEO and a Google Maps embed." Not a feature dump. Connection.

12–15 min · Anchor & Close

Name the price range, commit to a next step

"For something like this, we'd be looking at around ₹12,999 — I'll send you a detailed written proposal within an hour. Does that timeline work?"

Never end without a committed next step. "I'll be in touch" is not a next step.

15–20 min · Buffer

Handle one objection if it comes up

If they raise price or timeline, address it directly. Don't dodge. Use the objection scripts in Phase 4. If it's complex, say "Let me address this in the proposal — I'll show you exactly what you get for that investment."

Discovery questions — keep these handy

  • "How do most of your current clients find you — online or offline?"
  • "Do you have a website already? If yes, what feels broken about it?"
  • "What does a successful website look like for you — what would it do for your business?"
  • "Have you worked with any agencies before? What was that experience like?"
  • "If the website is live in 2 weeks — what changes for your business?"
  • "Who is your ideal customer? What do they search for when they need you?"
  • "What are your 2–3 biggest competitors doing online that you'd like to match or beat?"

Post-call message (send within 60 minutes)

📲 Post-Call Summary — WhatsApp Great speaking with you, [Name]! 🙌 Here's a quick recap of what we discussed: 🎯 Goal: [e.g., Get more student enquiries through Google & WhatsApp] 🛠️ Solution: [e.g., 5-page website — Home, About, Courses, Admission, Contact] 💰 Estimated investment: ₹[X] 📅 Timeline: [X] days from start date I'll send you a detailed proposal within the next hour that includes exactly what's covered, the payment structure, and the start date. Any questions in the meantime — just reply here 🙂

Proposal & Pricing Strategy

A proposal that closes — not a document that creates confusion.

Proposal structure — 5 elements only

  • Problem restatement — Reflect back what they told you. Shows you listened. Makes it personal.
  • Recommended solution — Name the package. List deliverables in plain language (not jargon).
  • Single clear investment — One number, not a range. Ranges invite negotiation.
  • Exact timeline — Specific dates. "By [Date]" not "approximately 7 days".
  • Clear next step — "Reply YES and I send the payment link + kickoff form." Frictionless.

Pricing anchor strategy

Always show 3 options. The middle package is what you want them to pick. The top package makes the middle feel reasonable. The bottom gives them an "escape hatch" without losing the deal.

Tip: Lead with Growth. If they push back on price, drop to Starter. If they're clearly premium, anchor with Authority first to make Growth feel like a steal.

Objection Handling

Every common pushback — with the exact words to handle it.

"It's too expensive / bahut mehnga hai."
"I understand completely. Let me put it in context: ₹12,999 is about ₹1,100/month over a year. If the website brings in even one new student or client per month — at any fee — you've paid for it many times over. Most of our clients see their first lead within the first week of launch. Would a smaller package make more sense to start — we have a Starter at ₹6,999 with a 3-day turnaround?"
"I need to think about it / soch ke batata hoon."
"Completely fair — take the time you need. Can I ask one thing: is it the budget, the timing, or something about the proposal that needs more clarity? Even one specific concern — I'll address it directly and it'll make your decision much easier."
"Can you show me examples first?"
"Absolutely — check out our projects page: sparkedge-agency.github.io/projects.html. Each project shows the live site, the client type, and the result. Which industry is closest to yours? I'll pull the most relevant example for you right now."
"I'm also talking to other agencies."
"You should. Here's our edge: you talk directly to the founder (me) — no account managers, no juniors. Delivery in 3–7 days, not weeks. Written scope before we start, no hidden costs. And 50% payment only after delivery. Most agencies can't say all four. Happy to match timelines — take whatever time you need."
"Do I have to pay everything upfront?"
"No — we take 50% to begin and 50% only on delivery, after you've approved the final website. You don't pay the second half until you've seen it and said yes. Zero risk on your side."
"What if I don't like what you build?"
"Great question — every package includes a revision round. Growth includes 1 month of free changes after launch, Authority includes 3 months. And you'll see a live preview link before anything goes public — you approve it before it's live. If something's wrong, I fix it. Simple."
"My friend said he can make one for free."
"He probably can build something. The question is: will it load fast, rank on Google, look professional on mobile, and actually convert visitors into enquiries? Free usually means Wix/WordPress with generic templates that look like everyone else. What we build is custom-coded, conversion-optimised, and SEO-ready. If the site brings in one extra student, it's paid for itself."
"I already have a website."
"That's great — mind if I take a quick look? [Ask for the URL.] I often spot 2–3 things that are quietly costing businesses leads — slow speed, no WhatsApp CTA, weak mobile layout. If your site is already working well, I'll tell you honestly. If there's room to improve, I'll show you exactly what I'd change."

Payment tracking — what to record

  • Client name + business name
  • Package + total amount
  • 50% advance — amount + date received + UPI ref ID
  • Start date + delivery date
  • 50% balance — amount + date received (on delivery)
  • Any add-ons ordered + their status

Tool: Use a simple Google Sheet titled "SparkEdge Projects" with one row per client. See the AI Prompts section to auto-generate this sheet structure.

Payment protection rules

  • Never start work without receiving the advance. "I'll pay after I see the work" is a red flag — don't agree.
  • Never deliver final files before receiving the remaining 50%. The preview link is fine. But final source code and handover happen after full payment.
  • Get confirmation in writing. WhatsApp messages count. Screenshot everything.
  • If a client ghosts after advance: Wait 7 days, send two polite follow-ups, then archive the project. You keep the advance per your terms.

Onboarding & Project Kickoff

Collect everything you need upfront. Never chase clients for content mid-project.

Kickoff form — every field you need

Create this as a Google Form. Link it in every payment confirmation message.

  • Business name — Full legal name + display name if different
  • Tagline / one-liner — What they do in one sentence
  • About the business — 2–3 paragraph description (free text)
  • Logo file — PNG with transparent background (Google Drive link)
  • Brand colours — Hex codes, or tick "No preference — SparkEdge chooses"
  • 3 reference websites — Style/design they like (URLs)
  • Services/courses/products list — Name + short description each
  • Pricing (if applicable) — To show on site or not
  • Contact info for site — Phone, email, address, WhatsApp number
  • Team/faculty photos — Google Drive link
  • Social media links — Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, etc.
  • Target audience — Who visits the site, what do they want to know
  • Any other notes — Free text field for anything else

Kickoff confirmation message

📲 Kickoff Started — WhatsApp Form received — thank you! We're officially building 🔥 Here's your project plan: 📋 SCOPE LOCKED [List exactly what's included — page by page] 📅 TIMELINE • Design begins: Today, [Date] • First draft / preview: [Date] • Final delivery: [Date] 📬 HOW WE COMMUNICATE • I'll send you a progress update every 2 days • You can message me anytime — I respond within 2–3 hours • Revisions collected in one round after preview If you have additional files (photos, logos) — share them in a Google Drive folder and drop the link here. Let's make something great 🚀

Scope lock — prevent scope creep

Scope creep (clients adding pages, features or "small" requests after the project starts) is the #1 cause of unprofitable projects. Prevent it at kickoff:

  • Always list exact pages and features in your kickoff confirmation (not "a website" — "5-page website: Home, About, 3 service pages, Contact")
  • If they ask for something outside the original scope mid-project, say: "That's a great idea — it's just outside the original scope. I can add it for ₹1,500 and deliver it 2 days after the main site. Want me to include it?"
  • Never say yes to extra work "just this once" without charging — it sets a precedent

The 4 levers to scale revenue

  • Lever 1 — Raise prices. Every 3 months, increase each package by ₹1,000–₹2,000. Your skills improve; your price should too. No existing client is affected — this is for new inquiries only.
  • Lever 2 — Add-ons. Every project should include at least one add-on conversation. Google Analytics, domain setup, monthly maintenance — these are easy yes's that add ₹1,500–₹3,000 per project.
  • Lever 3 — Recurring retainers. 5 maintenance clients at ₹1,500/month = ₹7,500 guaranteed every month before you take on a single new project.
  • Lever 4 — Referral system. Once you have 10 happy clients, you should get 2–3 referrals/month passively. Referrals close at 60–70% vs 10–15% for cold outreach.

Systems to build as you scale

  • Project tracking sheet: Google Sheets — one row per client. Columns: Name | Business | Package | Advance | Balance | Status | Delivery Date | Notes
  • Template library: Save every proposal, message and checklist in one Google Doc. Copy-paste, don't retype.
  • Asset folder system: One Google Drive folder per client. Inside: Kickoff Form Responses / Client Files / Final Deliverables / Project Notes
  • Weekly review (30 min every Sunday): How many leads in? Proposals sent? Projects started? Revenue collected? What's behind? What's next?
  • Portfolio update cadence: Add every finished project to your website within 3 days of delivery. Fresh portfolio = more trust = higher conversion.

When to hire / delegate (₹70K+ months)

  • You're turning down projects due to capacity → it's time to bring in a junior (batch-mate, college student) for ₹3,000–₹5,000/project
  • You're spending more than 2 hours/week on Instagram content → outsource content creation
  • Clients keep asking for things outside your skill set (backend, app) → build a referral network with complementary developers; earn a cut
  • Rule: Delegate everything that doesn't require your direct client relationship or your design judgment. Keep: client calls, proposals, project oversight. Delegate: content updates, social posts, basic HTML edits.
