The 5 Strategies

5 Sure-Shot Ways to Close Your First Client

These work specifically for the Indian market — coaching institutes, schools and local businesses.

1

The "Free Audit" Strategy — Your Fastest Path to ₹12,999

Find a coaching institute with a bad or no website. Message them offering a FREE 10-minute website audit. On the call, show them 3 specific problems. End with: "We can fix all of this for ₹12,999. Should I send you the proposal now?"

Why it works: You lead with value, not a sales pitch. They already know their website is bad — you're just confirming it with a solution in hand.

Hi [Name], I just visited [Institute Name]'s website and noticed 3 things that are likely costing you student enquiries — slow loading, not mobile-friendly, and no clear admission form. I'm Raunak from SparkEdge. We build high-conversion websites specifically for coaching institutes. Would you be open to a free 10-minute review where I show you exactly what we'd fix? No charge. No obligation. Just value. Our work: https://sparkedge-agency.github.io
2

The "Local Search Shame" Strategy — Hits Where It Hurts

Google "[city] coaching institute" — screenshot the top results. Message prospects who are not on page 1: "Your competitors are getting enquiries from Google. You're not showing up at all." Every business owner fears losing to a competitor more than anything else.

Action: Search their name on Google. If they don't appear in top 5 for "[city] + [type]", that's your opener. Screenshot it and send it to them on WhatsApp.

3

The "Referral Machine" Strategy — 0 Cost, Compounding Returns

Tell every person you know: CA, doctor, teacher, college senior, parent — "If you know any coaching institute or business that needs a website, connect us. I'll give you ₹500 for every paid project." This network effect is underrated. One referral often leads to 3–4 more through the same community.

Script: "Hey [Name], I just launched a web design service for coaching institutes and small businesses. If you know any owner who needs a professional website, please connect us — I offer ₹500 as a thank-you for every project that closes. 🙏"

4

The "WhatsApp Group Drop" Strategy — One Message, Multiple Leads

Post in local Facebook groups and WhatsApp communities for business owners. Don't pitch services — offer a free audit for 3 people. Scarcity + free value = responses.

🔍 FREE Website Audit for Coaching Institutes (3 spots only) I'm offering free 10-minute website audits for coaching institutes and local businesses this week. I'll review your current site (or explain why not having one is costing you students) and show you exactly what to fix to get more enquiries. → 3 spots only → Completely free → No sales pressure Comment "AUDIT" or DM me to grab a spot.
5

The "IIT Credibility" Strategy — Close at Premium Price

Lead every conversation with your IIT-ecosystem association. In India, this immediately signals intelligence, work ethic and trustworthiness — especially to education-sector clients who deeply respect IIT brands. Don't hide it. Make it your opening line.

Script: "Hi, I'm Raunak from SparkEdge. We're an IIT-ecosystem team that builds websites specifically for coaching institutes. We've helped institutes get 3× more enquiries — I'd love to show you how."

Bonus close technique: When they hesitate on price, say: "One new student batch covers the website cost entirely. If we get you even 3 more enquiries a month, this pays for itself in under a week."

The Pathway

Onboarding to Delivery — Step by Step

Every step, every document, every message you need from first "yes" to live website.

Step 1 — Verbal Agreement

Client says "yes" on WhatsApp or call. Confirm verbally: package, price, 50% advance. Say: "Great! I'll send you a written proposal on WhatsApp right now. We start as soon as the advance is received."

Step 2 — Send Proposal (within 60 minutes)

Send the Project Proposal document on WhatsApp. Speed signals professionalism. Every hour you wait reduces close probability by ~15%.

Step 3 — Collect 50% Advance

Send your UPI QR or UPI ID. Do NOT start work before advance. This filters serious clients and gives you financial protection.

Step 4 — Send Onboarding Questionnaire

Send the Client Onboarding Form. Collect all content, preferences and requirements. Block 2–3 days for content collection from client side.

Step 5 — Design & Development (your side)

Build per the package scope. Send a daily 1-line WhatsApp update: "Day 2 update: Homepage and About page complete. On track for [delivery date]."

Step 6 — Share Preview Link

Deploy to GitHub Pages (live URL). Share for review. Ask for ONE consolidated feedback round — don't allow drip feedback.

Step 7 — Revisions + Final Approval

Complete revisions within agreed rounds. Get written confirmation ("approved" on WhatsApp) before final delivery.

Step 8 — Collect Balance + Deliver

Collect remaining 50%. Send all files + Site Management Guide. Ask for a testimonial immediately — success rate is highest in this moment.

Step 9 — Referral Ask (3 days after delivery)

Follow up: "Really glad you're happy with the website! If you know any other institute or business owner who needs something similar, I'd really appreciate the introduction. 🙏"

Required Documents

Every Document You Need — With AI Prompts

Use these prompts in Claude or ChatGPT to generate each document in seconds. Paste, customize with client details, send.

1. Project Proposal (Send immediately after agreement)

A clean 1-page WhatsApp message or PDF with: package name, deliverables, price, advance required, delivery date, and payment terms.

🤖 AI Prompt to Generate This: "Write a professional project proposal message for WhatsApp for a web design agency. Client: [NAME], Business: [BUSINESS NAME]. Package: Growth (₹12,999). Deliverables: 4-5 page website, mobile responsive, SEO setup, WhatsApp integration, 1 month support. Delivery: 7 days from advance payment. Payment: 50% advance (₹6,499) + 50% on delivery (₹6,500). UPI: [YOUR UPI]. Keep it concise, professional, and include a call-to-action to send the advance. Sign off as Raunak, SparkEdge."

2. Client Onboarding Questionnaire (Send after advance received)

Collect: business name, tagline, target audience, services list, photos/logos, color preferences, competitor URLs, and any specific features required.

🤖 AI Prompt to Generate This: "Create a client onboarding questionnaire for a web design agency building a website for a coaching institute. Include questions about: business name and tagline, target students (age/exam), list of courses/programs offered, USPs vs competitors, existing brand colors or preference, logo (or do they need one), team/faculty photos, testimonials they want to show, 2-3 competitor websites they like or dislike, contact details to display, any specific pages they want, and deadline urgency. Format as a clean numbered list suitable for WhatsApp or Google Form."

3. Simple Service Agreement (Protects you legally)

A brief written agreement covering: scope, timeline, payment terms, revision rounds, ownership rights, and what happens if client delays content.

🤖 AI Prompt to Generate This: "Draft a simple, plain-English service agreement for a freelance web design project in India. Parties: SparkEdge (service provider) and [CLIENT NAME] (client). Include: project scope (4-5 page website), payment schedule (50% advance, 50% on delivery), delivery timeline (7 working days after content received), number of revision rounds included (2), intellectual property (client owns final deliverables after full payment), content responsibility (client provides all text/images on time), delay clause (if client delays content by 5+ days, timeline resets), and dispute resolution (amicable resolution first). Keep it under 400 words. Suitable to share as a WhatsApp message or simple PDF."

4. Payment Receipt / Invoice

A simple receipt to send after each payment — looks professional and builds trust, especially for the advance payment.

🤖 AI Prompt to Generate This: "Create a simple professional invoice/receipt template for a web design agency in India. Include: Invoice number, Date, From: SparkEdge (sparkedge555@gmail.com, +91 91424 50715), To: [CLIENT NAME, BUSINESS], Service: [Package Name] Website Development, Amount Received: ₹[AMOUNT], Payment Method: UPI, Balance Due: ₹[AMOUNT], Note: 'Work begins upon receipt of advance. Balance due upon delivery and approval.' Make it clean and professional, suitable to share as text on WhatsApp."

5. Site Management Guide (Send at delivery)

A simple guide explaining: how to edit text, how to add/remove pages, how to update contact info, and how to reach SparkEdge for support.

🤖 AI Prompt to Generate This: "Write a simple Website Management Guide for a non-technical small business owner who just received their website from SparkEdge. The website is built with HTML/CSS/JS hosted on GitHub Pages. Cover: 1) How to request changes (WhatsApp SparkEdge), 2) How to update the GitHub repository files using the web interface (drag and drop), 3) How to change basic text if they're comfortable with HTML, 4) How to add a new page (brief), 5) Support contact and response time, 6) Domain renewal reminder (yearly). Keep it friendly, non-technical and under 500 words."

6. Testimonial Request Message (Send 3 days post-delivery)

A friendly request for a Google Review or WhatsApp testimonial that you can use on your website.

🤖 AI Prompt to Generate This: "Write a warm, non-pushy WhatsApp message from a web designer to a happy client, asking for a brief testimonial. The client's website was just delivered and they're happy with it. The message should: thank them for their trust, mention that testimonials help small agencies grow, ask for 2-3 sentences about their experience (what problem it solved, what they liked), and optionally ask for a Google review. Keep it under 100 words. Signed as Raunak, SparkEdge."
Quick Checklists

Pre-Delivery Checklists

✅ Before You Start Work

  • 50% advance received in your account
  • Onboarding questionnaire filled by client
  • Logo file received (PNG/SVG)
  • All text content received or confirmed you'll write it
  • Delivery date communicated and agreed
  • Service agreement acknowledged (even informally)

✅ Before You Deliver

  • All pages mobile-responsive tested on real phone
  • Contact form / WhatsApp button tested and working
  • Page load speed tested (Google PageSpeed Insights)
  • SEO meta titles and descriptions added to all pages
  • Client's phone number / email correct throughout
  • All images compressed and loading correctly
  • Balance payment collected before file handover
