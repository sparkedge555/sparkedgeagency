1 First Contact & Lead Qualification

When a lead messages you on WhatsApp

Respond within 30 minutes during business hours. Speed is your #1 trust signal. Use this template:

📲 First Reply — WhatsApp Hi [Name]! 👋 Thanks for reaching out to SparkEdge. I'm Raunak — I personally handle all projects here. Quick question to get started: What kind of website are you looking for, and what's your business? (e.g., coaching institute, clinic, personal brand) This helps me give you a proper recommendation right away. 🚀

Qualify the lead in 3 questions

Before investing time, confirm they are a real fit. Ask these:

  • What's your business? — Coaching? School? Clinic? Shop? This tells you the package and effort needed.
  • What's your timeline? — "Needed ASAP" = motivated buyer. "Just exploring" = nurture them.
  • Do you have a budget in mind? — Not mandatory, but it filters serious buyers from time-wasters.

After qualifying — move fast

Once you know they're serious, suggest a short call immediately. Use this:

📲 Book a Call — WhatsApp Got it! Based on what you've shared, I have a clear picture already. Let me hop on a quick 15-minute call with you — no sales pitch, just a straight conversation about what you actually need and whether we're a good fit. When works for you today or tomorrow? I'm free most times. 📞

2 Discovery Call Script

Call structure (15–20 minutes)

0–2 min

Warm opener

"Thanks for the time — I'll keep this short. Tell me a bit about your business. How long have you been running it, and what's going well for you right now?"

2–7 min

Diagnose the problem

"What made you reach out now? Are you getting enquiries from anywhere at the moment — Google, Instagram, word of mouth?" Listen more than you talk. Let them describe the gap.

7–12 min

Present the solution (briefly)

"Based on what you've told me, here's exactly what I'd build for you and why..." Mention 2–3 specific features, not a laundry list. Connect each to their stated problem.

12–15 min

Anchor & close next step

"For something like this, we'd be looking at around ₹X–₹Y. I'll send you a written proposal today. Sound good?" Never end a call without a committed next step.

Key discovery questions to keep handy

  • "How do most of your current clients find you?"
  • "Do you have a website already? What do you feel is missing?"
  • "What would a successful website look like to you — what would it do for your business?"
  • "Have you looked at other agencies? What's held you back from moving forward?"
  • "If we get the website live in the next 2 weeks, what changes for you?"

Post-call follow-up (send within 1 hour)

📲 Post-Call Follow-Up — WhatsApp Great speaking with you, [Name]! 🙌 As promised, here's a quick summary of what we discussed: ✅ Your goal: [e.g., get more student enquiries online] ✅ What we'd build: [e.g., 5-page coaching website with lead form + SEO] ✅ Estimated investment: ₹[X] ✅ Timeline: [X] days from start I'll send a detailed proposal shortly. Any questions in the meantime?

3 Proposal & Pricing

Proposal structure (keep it short & clear)

  • Problem restatement — Reflect back what they told you. This shows you listened.
  • Recommended solution — Name the package and exactly what's included.
  • Investment — Give a single clear number. Not a range. Ranges invite negotiation.
  • Timeline — Specific dates, not vague "about 7 days".
  • Next step — "Reply with YES and I'll send the payment link and kickoff form."

Pricing anchor strategy

Always present 3 options. The middle package is what you want them to pick — it anchors the price conversation and makes the premium feel reasonable.

4 Objection Handling

"It's too expensive."
"I understand — let me put it in context. ₹12,999 is roughly ₹1,100/month over a year. If the website brings in even one new student/client per month, you've paid for it many times over. Most of our clients see their first lead within the first week. Would a smaller starting package work better — we have a starter at ₹6,999?"
"I need to think about it."
"Completely fair. Can I ask — is it the budget, the timing, or something about the proposal that needs more clarity? I want to make sure I'm giving you exactly what you need to make a good decision. Even one specific concern I can address?"
"Can you show me examples first?"
"Absolutely — check out our projects page: [link to projects.html]. You can see real websites we've built. Each one has client feedback directly below it. Which industry is closest to yours? I'll pull the most relevant one."
"I'm talking to other agencies too."
"Good — you should compare. Here's our edge: you talk directly to the founder (me), not an account manager. Delivery in 3–7 days, not weeks. And a clear written scope before we start — no hidden charges. Most agencies can't say all three. Happy to match timelines so you have what you need to decide."
"Do I need to pay everything upfront?"
"We take 50% upfront to begin, 50% on delivery. You pay the second half only after you've seen the final website and approved it. No risk on your side."
"What if I don't like it?"
"All packages include a revision round — we refine until you're happy. Growth includes 1 month of free changes, Authority includes 3 months. And we share a live preview before anything goes live, so you approve it before it's public."

5 Project Kickoff & Onboarding

Once they confirm — move within 24 hours

  • Send payment link (UPI / bank transfer)
  • Send kickoff form (Google Form) — collect all content, logo, colours, references
  • Confirm start date and expected delivery date in writing
  • Send a brief project summary message recapping scope

Kickoff form — what to collect

  • Business name, tagline, and description (2–3 paragraphs)
  • Logo file (PNG with transparent background preferred)
  • Brand colours (hex codes, or "no preference")
  • 3 reference websites they like (for style direction)
  • Services/courses/products list with descriptions
  • WhatsApp number, email, address for contact page
  • Team/faculty photos if needed (Google Drive link)
  • Any existing social media links
  • Target audience (who visits the site, what they want)

Kickoff confirmation message

📲 Kickoff Confirmation — WhatsApp Payment received — we're officially started! 🎉 Here's what happens next: 📋 Step 1: Please fill this form with your content: [Your Google Form link here] 📅 Step 2: I'll begin design on [Start Date] 🚀 Step 3: You'll get a live preview link by [Delivery Date] If you have any logos, photos or files to share — drop them in a Google Drive folder and share the link here. Any questions at any point, just message me directly. Let's build something great 🔥

Launch & handover checklist

When to push up vs. down

  • Push Growth → Authority when: They mention multiple locations, premium brand, hospital or school with 500+ students. Frame it as "for an institution your size, you really want 6–8 pages and 3 months of support".
  • Push Starter → Growth when: They ask for more than 1 page, or mention wanting "leads". A 1-page site rarely converts as well.
  • Drop to Starter when: Budget objection is real and they're otherwise a great fit. A ₹6,999 client who refers 3 more is worth more than a lost deal.
